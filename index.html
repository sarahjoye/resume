About Me

I am a business student pursuing a career in social entrepreneurship. I am interested in all the ways that business, government, and nonprofit work can be combined to effectively address social issues. I have 4 years of experience in the social work and nonprofit world. As a certified advocacy services provider for victims of sexual assault, I have learned to keep a clear head in a crisis, recognize and meet unspoken needs, and provide trauma-informed care. I currently work as a behavioral technician in a residential care center for women with eating disorders. Here I have developed strong leadership and interpersonal skills and increased my ability to provide compassionate care. I have spent time teaching English to migrants in my community, volunteering for a Children's Justice Center, and interacting with senior citizens. I also spent a year and half living in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland as a volunteer for my church, where I became more familiar with relevant European social issues, such as the refugee crisis, gender and racial equality, and sustainable living practices. Read my research paper about German refugee integration here.

In addition to my experience in the nonprofit world, I've been involved in sales and spent a summer selling solar panels door to door. There I gained negotiation skills, grit, and resilience. I am interested in expanding my repertoire of hard business skills further and recently put time into learning Tableau, VBA Excel, Flowcharts, HTML, CSS, and SQL (I made this page from scratch). I am studying Experience Design and Management in the Brigham Young University school of business and am minoring in Global and Community Impact. In this minor, we focus on measuring and evaluating impact, leveraging organizations to build coalitions, and human-centered design. I hope my skills in these areas will be useful to your organization.

Areas of particular interest to me include refugee/migrant issues, children's issues, racial/ethnic equality, environmental issues, education, or any startup organization. My ultimate career goal is to leverage my education and experience to make a positive, measurable impact on the lives of individuals and communities in need. I look forward to learning from and working with you.
